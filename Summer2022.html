Work Term: Summer 2022

IT Support at the University of Guelph

May 18, 2022 to August 26, 2022

Employer Information

My employer was Physical Resources of the University of Guelph. I assisted with the information technology (IT) department of Physical Resources. Physical Resources builds, operates, and maintains physical assets of the University. They provide support services to the entire Campus.

Goals

I had a goal to improve my skills and understanding of 3D printing technology. I worked on this goal by practicing many Fusion360 functions. I was able to create models quickly while maintaining quality. Another thing I learned for this goal was multi-colour 3D prints. Within the 3D model slicing software, I learned a script to pause the 3D printing at a predefined layer. I then removed the current filament and replaced it with a different colour. One application of this was creating QR codes. QR codes need contrasting colours for devices to scan the code. I received a new 3D printer to take home for personal use. I learned how to assemble and maintain my 3D printer. These situations aided in the completion of this goal.

My goal was to improve my oral communication. To improve on this goal, I tried to ask questions about tasks if I could not find a solution or for clarification. Also, I would give my opinion on issues to help facilitate critical thinking. Although these situations aided progress towards my goal, I would still like to improve my oral skills as it is the area that needs the most development.

I also had a goal to apply my knowledge of ASP.NET, which I learned from my last work term. I received a task to search through an ASP.NET code base and fix an issue that occurred in the program. I was able to locate where the problem was and created code to solve it. I then sent my edits to the live website to confirm that the solution worked. I am glad I can apply the knowledge I learned in my last work term.

Job Description

Some basic tasks that I've done in my work term are:

  • - On-Call Technology Assistance
    • - Software or Hardware Issues
    • - Software Installations
  • - Software and Hardware Upgrades/Updates
  • - Workstation Installations
  • - Printer Setup
  • - Device Setup
    • - Desktops and Laptops
    • - Mobile Phones
    • - UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply)
  • - Asset Management

These basic tasks required critical thinking and problem-solving. Setup tasks required the following procedures to ensure a standard. Communication was also needed with users to set up a meeting.

I was assigned the task of designing and creating 3D prints. This task allowed me to learn how to use a modeling program called Fusion 360 to create models. I then used Simiplify3D to convert my models into files read by a 3D printer to print out the model. It helped improve my problem-solving skills as there are many variables to 3D printing to create successful prints.

Conclusions

In all, this was an enjoyable work term where I was able to expand my understanding, met great people, and I progressed further with my goals. Thank you for reading my reflections upon this work term.
