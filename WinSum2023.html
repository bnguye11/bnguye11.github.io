Work Term: Wubter 2023 - Summer 2023

Student IT Technician at the University of Guelph - Computing & Communications Services

Jan 3, 2023 to Sept 1, 2023

Employer Information

My employer was the Computing and Communications Services (CCS) of the University of Guelph. CCS assists many different departments throughout the University with information technology, information security, file databases, and many more. I assisted with the Managed Desktop department of CCS for a 8-month work term. At Managed Desktops our overall responsibilities were to support endpoint users. With devices that are under the University's Group policy.

Goals

I had a goal to improve my oral communication skills. Oral communication is used widely in any workplace, with colleagues or clients. I usually try to solve the issue myself, but some problems are solved faster if I ask questions when I am stuck. My goal is to improve my oral communication skills and be able to provide meaningful input in conversations. To work on this goal I asked questions after an appropriate time working on an issue by myself. I talked to clients to identify the problems they are encountering. Throughout the work term, I got many opportunities to work with clients and was able to communicate what I am doing to help them resolve the issue, allowing them to understand the process of fixing the issue. I try to complete tasks without much assistance from my colleagues. When I get to a roadblock that I can't overcome, I asked for assistance and troubleshoot. I feel more confortable talking to others, but communication will always be a skill that can be improved upon

My goal was to improve my technological literacy. Many programs and systems are usually unfamiliar in a new workplace. I aim to understand the technologies and systems CCS utilize in their workplace. I have played around with the program and systems that the CCS use. Since I used their technologies every day I was able to improve my knowledge about them. I knew I was successful when I was able help new co-ops with the ticketing system and group policy systems that was used. Overall, I was able to improve my technological literacy and I am ready to improve it by learning new software and systems.

A common issue in IT is identifying, troubleshooting, and solving problems. My goal is to improve my problem-solving skills. I asked questions to my colleagues to learn solutions to common issues and studied previous cases and applied their solutions to client's problems. I was able to improve my problem solving skills and hope to continue to develope it.

Job Description

Some basic tasks that I've done in my work term are:

  • - On-Call Technology Assistance
    • - Software or Hardware Issues
    • - Software Installations
  • - Software and Hardware Upgrades/Updates
  • - Workstation Installations
  • - Printer Setup
  • - Device Setup
    • - Desktops and Laptops
    • - Mobile Phones
    • - UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply)
  • - Asset Management

These basic tasks required critical thinking and problem-solving. Setup tasks required the following procedures to ensure a standard. Communication was also needed with users to set up a meeting.

Another task that was commonly given was to build devices. The computer was connected to the university's internet and booted through PXE to receive files to setup the device. It will run through WinPE to install the necessary software and set the group policy on the laptop. Once the process is finish, I log onto the device and install Dell Command | Updates and Windows updates. Once that is complete, I message the client that their laptop is ready for pickup

<

Decommissioning devices is a task that comprised of wiping all storage components and destroying them afterwards, everything is then placed into E-waste. A ticket is then created and assigned to the analyst to remove the device from Microsoft Endpoint Configuration Manager, Active Directory, and the anti-virus software.

Conclusions

In all, this was an enjoyable and welcoming work term where I was able to expand my understanding, meet great people, and I progressed further with my goals. Thank you for reading my reflections upon this work term.
